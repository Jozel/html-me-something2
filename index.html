Trip to Amsterdam

Count down

MY trip to Amsterdam started with some scary news. My friends and I have been planning to travel to Amsterdam for months before we left. The week before our flight we got news that Hurricane Irma was heading our way and it was predicted to hit Florida the day we were planning to leave. This caused feelings of frantic and confused. We were not sure that we should leave at such an uncertain time. Our families were worried and South Florida was in disarray.

We decided to get together a few days before to see how we all felt about the situation. We concluded that it made no sense for us to be here during the storm since we had to evacuate anyways. therefore, we all agreed that if we could leave as scheduled we would all go. Fortunately for us Irma’s path made a slight turn and it was predicted to hit land the day after our flight. We were all relieved but very worried at the same time. Our Europe trip was supposed to be excitement and adventure but instead it was turning into feel more like anxiety and uncertainty. The day of departure was here, and we were all a bit scared. We all prepared ourselves and our homes for the worst and prayed for the best. we rounded up our courage. Said fair wale to South Florida. I remember talking to our families from the airport and making sure everyone was okay and prepared. The whole 15-hour trip to Amsterdam was dragging and every chance we got we were on our phones watching the news and texting our folks.

Day of arrival

We arrived in Amsterdam at 11am a day after we departed. The day was beautiful, and you can feel that the vibe around us was different. We were all a bit cultured shocked but excided at the same time. As we arrived everyone around us was speaking Dutch. The information signs were different, and you can feel the difference in culture. Everyone was going about their business while we all stud in the airport as a group looking around, reading signs and trying to figure out what to do next. By then Irma had already hit land and we had no communication with our families. The power was down back home, our families were hunkered down, and we were left wondering if we did the right thing. Until one of us blurred out the fact that we would be hiding in a boarded-up house, with winds and heavy rain. With no power and no AC. Why not be here instead. We all looked at each other and smiled. One of us said I can’t believe we are here right now. I agreed and we all started throwing suggestions on what to do next. One of us started walking towards a sign and said out loud let’s exchange currency and find our way to the hotel.

Our stay

By the next day we were able to reach our families and it turned out everyone was okay. This provided us relieve and allowed for us to enjoy ourselves. The Hotel we stayed in was in the west out skits of Amsterdam.

We traveled by bus and train from the hotel to the city during our stay. The out skits were charming, and you can really get the feel of life and culture of the locals.

We tried all the nearby café’s and food around the area. The travel to the city by local transportation was about 15 minutes and it gave us a sense of local commute. Amsterdam is an exhilarating city with a lot of culture and history. We checked out the amazing architecture and famous sites. On one excursion we bought tickets for the hop on hop off cruise. The cruise was very convenient to get to know the whole city in a short period of time. We traveled through the all the canals and learned insight into the history of Amsterdam. We often got off and explored different parts of the city and hopped back on the next stop.

We checked out the Heineken tour and enjoyed its history. At the end of the Heineken tour there is a bar with a live DJ and drinks included. It was nightlife in the middle of the day.

We also enjoyed the Red-light district. Its is a very vibrant and lively part of the city filled with some interesting characters.During our trip we meet travelers from all over the world. We made a new friend from Cairo, Egypt and meet some folks from San Francisco. People in Amsterdam are friendly and adventurous. They have an open mind about life and an attitude of do you and live life to the fullest.

End of trip

We had an amazing time in Amsterdam even though it started off as a scary situation. We traveled all over the city, tried the local foods and café’s and made great new friends. Over all Amsterdam was a wonderful experience and I would love to travel to the Netherlands again.
